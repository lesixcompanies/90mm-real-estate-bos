# Evaporating Cloud (EC)

## Purpose
Every constraint persists because of an underlying conflict — two things
the agent believes they need that appear to be mutually exclusive. The EC
makes that conflict explicit, identifies the hidden assumptions that
sustain it, and finds the assumption that can be challenged to break
the conflict.

## Structure
The EC has five elements:
- **A (The Objective):** What the agent ultimately wants
- **B (Need 1):** One thing required to achieve A
- **C (Need 2):** Another thing required to achieve A
- **D (Want 1):** The action that satisfies Need B
- **D' (Want 2):** The action that satisfies Need C
- **The Conflict:** D and D' appear to be mutually exclusive

## Process
1. After the diagnostic synthesis and resolution of Layers 0-2,
   present the EC to the agent:
   "Before we build anything, let me surface the conflict I see in
   your business."
2. Name A, B, C, D, and D' using the agent's own language from the
   diagnostic.
3. Identify the assumptions connecting each arrow (B→D, C→D', and
   especially the D↔D' conflict).
4. Ask the agent: "Which of these assumptions is worth challenging?
   Which one, if it weren't true, would break this conflict?"
5. The challenged assumption becomes the injection — the direction
   for change.

## How to Present It
Use plain language. Do NOT draw a formal diagram unless the agent asks
for one. Walk through it conversationally:

"Here's the conflict I see. You want [A]. To get there, you need [B],
which means you have to [D]. But you also need [C], which means you
have to [D']. And [D] and [D'] feel like they can't coexist because
you're assuming [assumption]. But what if that assumption isn't true?
What if [challenged assumption]?"

## Resistance Layer Addressed
- **Layer 3: "We disagree on the direction of the solution."**
  After presenting the injection, ask: "Does that feel like the right
  direction to you?"
  If NO → Explore different assumptions to challenge. The direction
  must be one the agent owns.
  Layer 3 must be resolved before building the FRT.

## Output
- The EC stated in plain language
- The assumptions identified
- The assumption being challenged (the injection)
- Agent's agreement with the direction (Layer 3 resolved)
- Save to diagnostics/ as part of the change sequence record
- Update context/constraints.md with EC findings

## What Happens Next
The injection from the EC becomes the hypothesis for the Future Reality
Tree. Move to FRT. Do NOT jump to execution.

## Example from Real Estate
- A: Build a sustainable, profitable practice
- B: Generate consistent inbound leads
- C: Serve current clients with full availability
- D: Invest time in content creation and marketing systems
- D': Be available to clients whenever they need me
- Conflict: D and D' can't both have all my time
- Assumption: Client responsiveness requires TOTAL availability
- Challenge: What if a 90-minute content block doesn't cost you clients?
- Injection: Establish a protected content creation window and test it
