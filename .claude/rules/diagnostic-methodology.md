# Diagnostic Methodology

This business operating system is built on constraint-based thinking using the
Theory of Constraints (TOC) Thinking Processes.

## Core Principle
At any given time, ONE factor limits the growth of this business more than
any other. That's the constraint. Everything else is noise until the
constraint is addressed.

## The Diagnostic Framework
The initial diagnostic explores the practice through four dimensions:
1. Market clarity — who you serve and how you think about your territory
2. Challenges — what's hard and what symptoms you experience daily
3. Barriers — what's preventing resolution (structural, financial, psychological, cultural)
4. Attempted solutions — what you've tried and what patterns emerge

These dimensions are explored through a natural conversation, not a checklist.
The constraint emerges from the pattern across all four.

## How This System Uses Constraint Thinking
- The diagnostic functions as a Current Reality Tree
- After identification, the change sequence validates and implements
- Weekly reviews check whether work is addressing the constraint
- Monthly reviews assess whether the constraint has shifted
- The decision log tracks whether decisions align with the constraint
- The diagnostic can be re-run when the constraint shifts

## Important
- Do not try to fix everything at once
- Work on the constraint first
- When the constraint is resolved, a new one will emerge — that's progress
- If you're spending time on things that don't address the constraint,
  ask yourself why before continuing
