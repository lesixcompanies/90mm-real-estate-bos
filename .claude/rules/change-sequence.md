# Change Sequence — When to Use Each Thinking Process

This file defines the order of operations for addressing any constraint
identified by the system. NEVER skip steps. NEVER jump to execution
without completing the sequence.

## The Three Questions
1. What to change? → Current Reality Tree + Evaporating Cloud
2. What to change to? → Future Reality Tree + Negative Branch Reservations
3. How to make change happen? → Strategy & Tactics Tree

## Layers of Legitimate Resistance

Every change encounters resistance. That resistance is not irrational —
it follows a predictable sequence of legitimate reservations that must
be resolved IN ORDER before the next layer can be addressed. If you skip
a layer, the unresolved reservation will resurface later and block
progress.

These layers are validation gates. The system does not advance past a
gate until the agent has genuinely resolved that layer's reservation.
Nodding along is not resolution. The agent must articulate agreement
or the system must address the disagreement before proceeding.

### "What to change?" — Layers 0, 1, 2

**Layer 0: "There is no problem."**
Gate: After the diagnostic synthesis. Before anything else.
Question to resolve: "Do you agree that what we've identified is a
real problem worth addressing?"
If NO → Stay in the CRT. Re-examine the evidence. The agent may need
to see the cost of inaction in concrete terms (lost listings, lost
revenue, compounding deferral). If they genuinely don't see a problem,
the system has nothing to work on. Acknowledge that and ask what they
would like to focus on instead.

**Layer 1: "We disagree on the problem."**
Gate: When stated constraint ≠ observed constraint.
Question to resolve: "The diagnostic suggests the problem might be
[observed], not [stated]. Here's why. Do you see it that way, or do
you see it differently?"
If disagreement → Do not steamroll. Work through the causal logic.
Show the cause-and-effect chain from the diagnostic. The agent may
have information you don't. If they present a compelling case, update
the constraint. If their case is the same pattern the diagnostic
already identified (e.g., restating symptoms as causes), name that
gently and explain why the diagnostic points deeper.
Resolution required before advancing to Layer 2.

**Layer 2: "The problem is out of my control."**
Gate: After agreement on what the problem is.
Question to resolve: "Do you believe this is something you can
actually influence, or does it feel like it's outside your control?"
If they say it's outside their control → Examine whether that's true
or whether it's a belief functioning as a barrier. Use the EC to
surface the assumption. Often "I can't control this" is itself the
assumption that needs challenging.
Resolution required before advancing to the EC.

### "What to change to?" — Layers 3, 4, 5

**Layer 3: "We disagree on the direction of the solution."**
Gate: After the EC produces an injection (the proposed direction).
Question to resolve: "The conflict suggests that if we challenge
[assumption], the direction would be [injection]. Does that feel
like the right direction to you?"
If NO → The agent may see a different assumption to challenge, or
a different direction entirely. Return to the EC assumptions and
explore alternatives. The direction must be one the agent owns,
not one the system imposed.
Resolution required before building the FRT.

**Layer 4: "We disagree on the details of the solution."**
Gate: During and after the FRT.
Question to resolve: "Here's what the future looks like if we go
this direction: [specific details with numbers and timelines from
the diagnostic]. Do you agree with these specifics? Is there
anything here that doesn't match how you see it playing out?"
If disagreement on details → Adjust the FRT. The agent knows their
market, their relationships, and their capacity better than the
system does. Modify the specifics until the future state is one the
agent recognizes and accepts as realistic.
Resolution required before moving to NBR.

**Layer 5: "Yes, but the solution has negative ramifications."**
Gate: NBR phase.
Question to resolve: "What could go wrong if we go this direction?
What concerns do you have?"
Process: For each concern raised, run a full NBR — name the negative
outcome, trace the cause, find the trimming action, validate the
trim. Every concern is legitimate until addressed.
If a negative branch cannot be trimmed → Return to the EC and
challenge a different assumption. The direction may need to change.
Resolution of ALL identified negative branches required before
advancing to the S&T Tree.

### "How to make change happen?" — Layers 6, 7, 8

**Layer 6: "Yes, but we can't implement the solution."**
Gate: After activation inventory is presented.
Question to resolve: "Here's everything that needs to exist before
we can execute. Looking at this list — is this feasible? Are there
items here that feel like real obstacles?"
If obstacles exist → Each obstacle becomes either (a) an intermediate
objective in the S&T Tree with its own strategy and tactic, or
(b) a reason to simplify the plan. If the agent looks at the full
scope and says "this is too much," the system should help identify
the minimum viable version that still addresses the constraint.
Resolution required before building the full S&T Tree.

**Layer 7: "We disagree on the details of implementation."**
Gate: During S&T Tree construction, at each intermediate objective.
Question to resolve: "Here's the next step in the plan: [IO]. The
approach would be [strategy] and specifically [tactic]. Does that
sequence make sense, or would you do it differently?"
If disagreement → Adjust. The agent may have practical knowledge
about their market, their schedule, or their tools that changes the
optimal sequence. The S&T Tree must reflect how the agent will
actually work, not how the system thinks they should work.
Every intermediate objective needs agent agreement before proceeding
to the next.

**Layer 8: "The solution is ultimately too risky and not worth it."**
Gate: After the complete S&T Tree is built, before execution begins.
Question to resolve: "Here's the full plan — the effort involved,
the timeline, the resources required, and the expected return. Looking
at all of it together: is this worth doing?"
If NO → This is not failure. The system should acknowledge that the
cost-benefit doesn't work for the agent right now. Options: simplify
the plan, defer with a specific trigger date, or return to the EC to
explore a lower-risk direction. The agent should never feel bulldozed
into execution they don't believe in.
Resolution required before execution begins.

## Critical Rules
- Layers must be resolved IN ORDER. Do not address Layer 5 concerns
  if Layer 3 hasn't been resolved.
- "I guess so" is not resolution. The agent should be able to
  articulate their agreement, not just fail to object.
- If the agent gets stuck at any layer, do not push past it. Stay
  at that layer and work through it. The layer IS the work.
- If resolution at any layer requires going back to a previous step
  (e.g., Layer 5 sends you back to the EC), that's the process
  working correctly, not a failure.
- Document which layers were resolved and any significant discussion
  at each gate in the diagnostics/ folder.

## Decision Logging Rubric

The system must recognize when a decision has been made and log it
immediately to decisions/log.md — not at the end of the session,
but at the moment the decision occurs.

### What Counts as a Decision
A decision is any of the following patterns:
- **Direction chosen:** "Let's do X instead of Y"
- **Money committed or declined:** "I'll invest in X" or "I'm not spending on Y"
- **Priority shifted:** "Actually, Z is more important right now"
- **Process created or modified:** A new process is documented or an existing one changes
- **Commitment made:** "I'll have this done by [date]" or "I'll talk to [person] about [thing]"
- **Tool or service adopted or dropped:** "I'm going to use X" or "I'm canceling Y"
- **Timeline set or changed:** A deadline is established, moved, or removed
- **Change sequence advancement:** Any layer resolution is itself a decision

### When to Log
Log the decision DURING the conversation, at the moment it happens.
Do not accumulate decisions and log them at the end. Each decision
gets its own entry.

### Format
[YYYY-MM-DD] DECISION: [what was decided]
REASONING: [why this decision was made]
CONSTRAINT ADDRESSED: [which constraint this relates to, or "none — [reason]"]
CONTEXT: [what was happening when this decision was made]

### If a Decision Doesn't Address the Constraint
This is not automatically wrong — sometimes legitimate work happens
outside the constraint. But it must be noted. Log WHY the decision
was made despite not addressing the constraint. Over time, the pattern
of off-constraint decisions is itself diagnostic data.

### Automatic Triggers
When any of these phrases or patterns appear in conversation, treat
them as decision signals and log immediately:
- "Let's go with..."
- "I've decided to..."
- "I'm going to..."
- "We should..."  (when followed by agreement/commitment)
- "Cancel / drop / stop [X]"
- "Start [X] by [date]"
- "I'll commit to..."
- Any Layer gate resolution during the change sequence
